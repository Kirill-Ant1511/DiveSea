Frequently Asked Question

What is an NFT?

An NFT, or non-fungible token, is a unique digital asset that represents ownership of a one-of-a-kind digital item. It's stored on a blockchain, a distributed ledger, which makes it tamper-proof and verifiable. Think of it as a certificate of ownership for a digital asset, like an image, video, or song.

What can I use NFTs for?

NFTs, or Non-Fungible Tokens, can be used for a wide range of purposes, extending beyond digital art and collectibles. They can represent ownership of digital and physical assets, provide access to exclusive events or communities, and even be used in DeFi and supply chain management.

What is the difference between an NFT and cryptocurrency?

The main difference between an NFT (Non-Fungible Token) and cryptocurrency lies in their fungibility and purpose. Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies that are fungible, meaning each unit is interchangeable with another (like dollars or euros) and can be used for transactions. NFTs, on the other hand, are unique, non-interchangeable tokens that represent ownership of a digital asset, like artwork or collectibles, and are not intended for use as currency.

How much is an NFT worth?
